Bernard L. Menezes has been on the faculty at IIT Bombay since October 1999. Earlier he was also an assistant professor in the Electrical Engineering Department and Institute for Advanced Computer Studies at the University of Maryland, College Park and was a visiting faculty member in the Computer Science Department at the University of New Mexico, Albuquerque.

His current research focus is in Computer Security and Applied Cryptography, including cache-based attacks on algorithms such as AES, DSA and EC-DSA, optimization of Elliptic Curve cryptographic operations and Web Security. His other research interests include parallel computing, smart E-Business and forecasting.

He earned a Ph.D. from the University of Texas at Austin, M.S. from the University of Notre Dame and a B.Tech. from IIT Bombay. Some of his recent research publications are:

  • Highly Efficient Algorithms for AES Key Retrieval in Cache Access Attacks (Extended version in Technical Report, Department of Computer Science and Engineering, IIT-Bombay)

  • Two for the price of one: A combined browser defense against XSS and clickjacking

  • Optimizing Elliptic Curve Scalar Optimization with Near-Factorization

He has authored a comprehnsive text book titlled "Network Security and Cryptography".
